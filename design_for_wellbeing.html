Design for the Well-Being of LGBTQIA+ University Students

About the Project

Designing for the LGBTQIA+ community in India with a focus on health is very much understudied. In India, the LGBTQIA+ community is very diverse, and prior research in Sociology has suggested that the community faces a lot challenges in the society. In this work, we are trying to understand how do LGBTQIA+ university students socialize and seek social support. We aim to answer "How do we design by involving the community and by leveraging existing human and material assets from within the community?". Therefore, we plan to conduct an interview study with LGBTQIA+ university students to understand what are different ways in which the students socialize and seek support.


Call for Participants

We invite university students who identify themselves as LGBTQIA+, or are still in closet, or questioning their identities/ expression; of all ages, gender identities, sexualities, religions, races, castes, and abilities to share their thoughts and experiences of seeking social and emotional support in their day-to-day life!

Eligible participants will be contacted via email/phone to set up a 30-50 minute online anonymous interview. Your inputs will help us to understand your views, concerns, and priorities around the different emotional & social support systems that exists in your life.
